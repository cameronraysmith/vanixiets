# Dagster primitives as a free-`Tasks`-plus-interpreter system

Each Dagster primitive maps onto either the free term or the interpreter of the Build Systems ├Ā la Carte construction, and naming which side a primitive lives on ŌĆö and how tightly it fits ŌĆö is what turns the analogy into a proof obligation.

The framing spine is *Build Systems ├Ā la Carte* (Mokhov, Mitchell, Peyton Jones, ICFP 2018, local copy at `~/projects/planning-workspace/engineering-references/mokhov-2018-build-systems-a-la-carte/`): a build is a fixpoint of `value(k) = task_k(values of deps(k))`, every system factors as `scheduler Ōłś rebuilder` over a `Tasks c k v` abstraction, and `c` is `Applicative` (static dependencies) or `Monad` (dynamic dependencies).
A `Tasks` value is a free structure (free applicative or free monad) over a signature of fetch-dependency operations; the build system is an interpreter ŌĆö an algebra, or a natural transformation ŌĆö that runs it against a store.
Most of Dagster maps onto *either the free term or the interpreter*, and confusing the two is the main source of muddle.

Each entry below states the exact Dagster API (verified against `~/projects/omicslake-workspace/dagster/python_modules/dagster/dagster/`, version `1!0+dev`), the categorical target, and a tightness tag.
A tag of *tight* means the structure is present and Dagster maintains it; *almost* means the structure holds exactly when a coherence condition Dagster does not enforce is met, and the entry names that condition.

## 1. The asset graph is the free diagram ŌĆö the `Tasks` object itself

The exact API is the `@asset` decorator (`_core/definitions/decorators/asset_decorator.py:156`), the global identity `AssetKey` (`_core/definitions/asset_key.py:33`, an ordered `path: Sequence[str]`), the dependency-declaration channels `deps: Iterable[CoercibleToAssetDep]` and `ins: Mapping[str, AssetIn]`, the multi-output `@multi_asset` (`asset_decorator.py:592`), and the pure descriptor `AssetSpec` (`_core/definitions/assets/definition/asset_spec.py:109`).
The resolved object is `AssetGraph` over `AssetNode`s, each carrying `parent_keys` and `child_keys`, exposing `asset_dep_graph`, `toposorted_asset_keys`, and `toposorted_asset_keys_by_level` (`_core/definitions/assets/graph/base_asset_graph.py`).

The categorical target is a free diagram: a functor from the free category (here a poset, since the dependency relation is a finite acyclic order) generated by the dependency DAG into the value category.
Objects are `AssetKey`s, morphisms are declared dependency edges, and the diagram is *free* in that it is generated purely by the declared edges with no imposed equations beyond those of a category.
This is the named/relational presentation of `Tasks`: where Mokhov's `Tasks c k v = k -> Maybe (Task c k v)` binds dependencies by a fetch callback inside each rule, Dagster names every node by a global `AssetKey` and declares edges between keys, so the whole diagram is reified as data rather than recovered by running a task.
The asset graph *is* the `Tasks` object ŌĆö the free term the rest of the machinery interprets.

This is tight.
The graph is fully static and topologically sortable, exactly the property Mokhov's topological scheduler demands of an applicative `Tasks` (┬¦4.1.1).
A non-input asset corresponds to `Just` a task, a `SourceAsset` (`source_asset.py:168`) to an input key (`Nothing`, user-supplied).
There is no coherence condition Dagster fails to enforce here: the resolved `AssetGraph` is acyclic (a declared cycle fails to topologically sort, via the `toposort` import from `dagster._core.utils`, and is rejected), the keys are global, and `toposorted_asset_keys` is the witness that the free diagram admits a linear extension.
The one subtlety worth stating is that the dependency edge carries identity, not data ŌĆö the framework matches by parameter-name-to-`AssetKey`, so the diagram is strongly typed in *identity* and only opt-in typed in *data* (see entry 3 and entry 7), but that does not weaken the diagram structure itself.

A refinement of this tight reading that real codebases force: the free term is often not authored directly but *computed* by an asset factory.
Production code rarely writes `@asset` by hand; it writes an effectful, cached, resource-dependent factory that constructs and returns the asset, check, and job definitions from configuration.
That construction is effectful, but the generated object is still exactly the free term of this entry ŌĆö the free diagram is unchanged, and only its construction is impure.
The factory's response is itself combined monoidally across factories, which is where this refinement connects to the combine/fold discipline of *03-fp-discipline-and-enforcement.md*.

## 2. Static versus dynamic is applicative versus monad ŌĆö the most load-bearing mapping

The exact API spans two layers because the distinction does too.
At the asset layer, the topology is the static `AssetGraph` of entry 1.
At the op layer, genuine value-dependent structure appears via `DynamicOut` (`_core/definitions/output.py:432`), `.map(...)` and `.collect()` on the invoked-node wrapper (`composition.py:718`/`:751`), expanded at run time through `UnresolvedMappedStepInput`/`UnresolvedCollectStepInput`/`ResolvedFromDynamicStepHandle` (`execution/plan/plan.py`).
At the partition-domain layer, `DynamicPartitionsDefinition` (`partitions/definition/dynamic.py`) adds partition *keys* at run time via `instance.add_dynamic_partitions(...)`.

The categorical target is the constraint hierarchy `Functor ŌŖé Applicative ŌŖé Selective ŌŖé Monad` from Mokhov ┬¦3 (the Task-constraint polymorphism of ┬¦3.4ŌĆō┬¦3.5; Selective is not in the 2018 source ŌĆö see the provenance caveat below).
`Task Applicative` means the dependency set is fixed by the *structure* of the task and is knowable without computing any value: the build system extracts the full graph ahead of time by running the task in a non-computing applicative, instantiating `f = Const [k]` so each fetch records its key and `Const`'s `<*>` concatenates them under the list monoid (Mokhov ┬¦3.7, `dependencies = getConst $ run task (\k -> Const [k])`).
`Task Monad` means `>>=` lets a *produced value choose the next dependency*, so the graph is not knowable in advance and can only be discovered by running against a concrete store.

The precise claim ŌĆö and the precision is the whole point ŌĆö is that the *asset-key topology is fully static*, applicative-flavored: the `AssetGraph` is the `Const`-extractable diagram of entry 1, resolved before any run, never altered by runtime data.
Genuine monadic structure (a produced value choosing the next dependency) lives strictly *below* the asset-key graph, in two places only.
First, op-level dynamic fan-out: inside the op that backs an asset, `DynamicOut`/`.map`/`.collect` make the *number* of parallel steps runtime-discovered while the *shape of the wiring template* stays static ŌĆö this is `>>=` at the step level, not the asset-key level.
Second, the partition domain: `DynamicPartitionsDefinition` grows an existing asset's partition key set at run time, again without changing the asset-key graph.
Net: there is no value-dependent asset-key edge in Dagster; the monadic fragment is op-step-level and partition-domain-level, and conflating it with the asset topology is the error this entry exists to forbid.

The Selective rung sits strictly between applicative and monad: `select :: f (Either a b) -> f (a -> b) -> f b` gives conditional execution where only one branch's *effects* run while *all branches' dependencies stay statically visible* as an over-approximation.
This is the rung Dagster's conditional and gating surfaces inhabit when they keep the full candidate-dependency set extractable but execute only a taken branch.
Three Dagster surfaces are Selective-flavored: `@multi_asset(can_subset=True)` (the framework may execute a statically-known subset), `AutomationCondition` gating (the dependency set is static; whether a partition is requested is decided per tick ŌĆö see entry 8), and partition selection through a `PartitionMapping` (the candidate upstream partitions are statically described; which subset is pulled is decided per downstream key ŌĆö see entry 4).

A provenance caveat is mandatory here: the local 2018 ICFP markdown *predates Selective*.
Selective functors were introduced in Mokhov et al., "Selective Applicative Functors" (2019) and folded into the JFP 2020 extended version of "Build Systems ├Ā la Carte"; the `select` signature and any Selective table row are absent from the 2018 copy.
Invoke Selective for the over-approximated-dependency reading, but flag it as reconstructed from the follow-on work, not quoted from the local source.

This mapping is tight, and it is the most load-bearing one: applicative-versus-monad is exactly the static-versus-dynamic axis, and the discipline of keeping the asset graph applicative (no value-dependent asset edges) is what makes the topological scheduler and early cutoff available at all.

## 3. The IO manager is the store side of the interpreter

The exact API is `IOManager` (`_core/storage/io_manager.py:130`, `class IOManager(InputManager, OutputManager)`) with `handle_output(self, context: OutputContext, obj: Any) -> None` storing an output and `load_input(self, context: InputContext) -> Any` loading it for a downstream consumer, plus the pydantic-config variant `ConfigurableIOManagerFactory` (`_config/pythonic_config/io_manager.py:86`).

The categorical target is the *interpreter*, specifically the fetch/store side of it.
Where the asset graph (entry 1) is the free term, the IO manager is the natural transformation that carries each free "I need dependency `k`" operation into an actual store effect: `load_input` realizes `fetch :: k -> f v` against external storage, `handle_output` realizes the store half.
It is the store algebra, or equivalently the effect handler interpreting the free structure ŌĆö the seam at which the named diagram becomes physical reads and writes.
This is Mokhov's `Store` plus the fetch callback, lifted out of the rule and made a swappable component, which is exactly the definition-versus-execution separation that licenses the whole BS├ĀlC reading.

This is *almost* tight, and the gap is the load-bearing part.
The IO manager is a lawful algebra exactly when two conditions hold, neither of which Dagster enforces.
First, `load_input Ōłś handle_output = id`: loading what was just stored must return the stored value, so the store round-trip is the identity on values.
Second, the storage address must be a *pure function of `(AssetKey, PartitionKey)`*: the same key (and partition) must always resolve to the same location, with no dependence on wall-clock time, run id, or ambient mutable state.
When both hold, materialization becomes a pure function of the key and the IO manager is a genuine store algebra; caching, early cutoff, and reproducibility follow.
Dagster does not check either condition: the transport is `Any`-typed by signature (`handle_output(..., obj: Any)`, `load_input(...) -> Any`), so there is no static or runtime guarantee that the loaded object even has the type the producer stored, let alone that the round-trip is the identity or that the address is key-determined.
This is the one component a practitioner typically writes themselves, so the law is a proof obligation, not a given.
The enforcement toolchain and a fully worked lawful IO-manager example are the subject of *03-fp-discipline-and-enforcement.md*.

## 4. Partitions are an indexed family fibered over partition space

The exact API is `PartitionsDefinition[T_str]` (`partitions/definition/partitions_definition.py`, abstract) with concrete subclasses `StaticPartitionsDefinition` (`static.py`), `TimeWindowPartitionsDefinition` (`time_window.py`) and its `Daily`/`Hourly`/`Weekly`/`Monthly` subclasses (`time_window_subclasses.py`), `MultiPartitionsDefinition` (`multi.py`, a Cartesian product of dimensions yielding `MultiPartitionKey`), and `DynamicPartitionsDefinition` (`dynamic.py`).
The per-edge index relation is `PartitionMapping` (`partitions/mapping/partition_mapping.py:38`, abstract) with `get_upstream_mapped_partitions_result_for_partitions(...)` (`:80`) and `get_downstream_partitions_for_partitions(...)` (`:48`); concrete mappers include `IdentityPartitionMapping`, `TimeWindowPartitionMapping`, `StaticPartitionMapping`, `LastPartitionMapping`, `AllPartitionMapping`, and `SpecificPartitionsPartitionMapping`.
Chunking is `BackfillPolicy` (`backfill_policy.py:18`) with `single_run()` (`:61`) and `multi_run(max_partitions_per_run=...)` (`:67`).

The categorical target is an indexed, fibered family of keys.
A partitioned asset is an indexed family `{v_p}_{p Ōłł P}`, equivalently a functor `P ŌåÆ V` from the partition index set into the value category ŌĆö a fibration of the asset over its partition space, where the fibre over each partition key `p` is that partition's value.
A `MultiPartitionsDefinition` is the product index `P = PŌéü ├Ś ŌĆ” ├Ś PŌéÖ`.
The categorical upgrade applies when the valid coordinates are *sparse* ŌĆö a real `model ├Ś dataset ├Ś grid` study rarely fills the full Cartesian product.
A sparse family is a *sub*-family of the full product: a presheaf on the sub-poset of valid coordinates, or equivalently a lazily-expanded family of keys defined over only the admissible index subset.
`PartitionMapping` is the morphism-level data on each dependency edge: a (partial) relation between the upstream and downstream partition-key index sets, pushed through during traversal by `get_parents_partitions`/`get_children_partitions` on the asset graph.
A backfill is applying the rebuilder over the sub-family: `BackfillPolicy` decides how the selected sub-family is chunked into runs, but *which* partitions to launch is computed from the static partition defs and mappings at launch time.

This is tight, with the sparse-product presheaf as the categorical refinement that makes the sparse-study case precise rather than approximate.

## 5. Materialization with `code_version` and `DataVersion` is the verifying or constructive trace

The exact API is `AssetMaterialization` (`_core/definitions/events.py:467`), the `code_version` parameter on `@asset`/`AssetSpec`, and `DataVersion` (`_core/definitions/data_version.py:39`).
Materializations are recorded in the event log keyed by `AssetKey` (plus partition), establishing the persistent queryable lineage record that the automation layer reads on each tick.

The categorical target is Mokhov's *trace* ŌĆö the cache theory of ┬¦4.2.
A trace is `Trace { key, depends :: [(k, Hash v)], result }`, and Dagster's record is exactly this: `code_version` contributes a hash of the rule, the input `DataVersion`s are the dependency hashes, and these hash together to a `DataVersion` for the output.
When the store holds only the hashes this is a *verifying trace* (┬¦4.2.2): rebuild iff a recorded input or code hash changed, which supports dynamic dependencies, minimality, and early cutoff.
When the store also holds the actual value (the materialized artifact addressed by the IO manager) it shades into a *constructive trace* (┬¦4.2.3): a cached value matching the current trace can be copied in without rebuilding, the basis for sharing across users.

Early cutoff is present *by its actual BS├ĀlC name*: when an asset re-materializes and its `DataVersion` is unchanged from the prior build, downstream assets whose recorded input version is that unchanged value need not re-execute ŌĆö the build stops propagating early (Mokhov ┬¦2.3, the canonical "comment added to `main.c`, `main.o` unchanged, `main.exe` not relinked").
This is exactly what the `DataVersion` machinery enables: a stable output version cuts off downstream recomputation.

This is tight; the trace structure and early cutoff are present under their BS├ĀlC names.
One can relate Dagster loosely to the `scheduler ├Ś rebuilder` grid (Mokhov Table 2) ŌĆö the verifying/constructive-trace rebuilder is clearly Dagster's, and the asset graph being topologically sortable suggests a topological scheduler ŌĆö but do not overclaim a single cell.
Dagster's scheduler choice depends on the executor and the automation layer rather than being fixed by the model, the rebuilder shades between verifying and constructive depending on whether the IO manager retains values, and the dynamic op fan-out complicates any clean topological reading.
The honest statement is that Dagster occupies the *trace-based rebuilder* region with early cutoff, not a single named cell of the grid.

## 6. Resources are a reader monad ŌĆö gesturally

The exact API is `ConfigurableResource` (`_config/pythonic_config/resource.py:597`, a Pydantic model whose fields are the config schema), injected into asset/op functions by parameter name or `required_resource_keys`.

The categorical target is the reader monad, or a coalgebra of ambient context: an asset function is a computation `Context ŌåÆ Value` that reads its database connections, clients, and configuration from an injected environment.
This is the functional-dependency-injection reading ŌĆö effects passed as explicit dependencies rather than reached for globally.

This is *almost*, and gesturally so: it is a descriptive vibe, not a structure whose laws Dagster checks.
There is no enforced purity of the read, no guarantee the resource is used only through the reader interface, and no law (such as commuting reads) that the framework verifies.
Use the reader-monad framing to *discipline yourself* ŌĆö keep the pure transformation core reading its effects from injected resources and isolate I/O at that boundary ŌĆö not as a claim about a structure Dagster maintains.

## 7. Asset checks are a runtime-checked refinement, not a ╬Ż-type

The exact API is `@asset_check` (`_core/definitions/decorators/asset_check_decorator.py:102`) producing an `AssetChecksDefinition`, with the runtime `AssetCheckResult`, the severity enum `AssetCheckSeverity` (`_core/definitions/asset_checks/asset_check_spec.py:30`, members `WARN` and `ERROR`), and the `blocking: bool` parameter (when true and severity is `ERROR`, downstream assets do not execute).
Checks are first-class graph nodes keyed by `AssetCheckKey`, distinct from `AssetKey`.

The categorical/type-theoretic target is a propositional refinement: a predicate `Žå` over an asset's value, conceptually `{v : Žä | Žå(v)}`, evaluated *post hoc* against the materialized artifact.
The proposition exists ŌĆö the check asserts `Žå(v)` and records pass or fail ŌĆö but the *static guarantee does not*.
A refinement type proper would make a value carry the proof of `Žå` in its type, so downstream code could rely on it without re-checking; an asset check instead runs `Žå` after materialization and reports a result.
It is therefore a *dynamically-checked refinement*, a design-by-contract runtime assertion, not a ╬Ż-type and not a refinement type: nothing in the type of the downstream asset's input reflects that the check passed.

This is *almost*: the contract is real and `blocking` even gates execution, but the guarantee is runtime and propositional, not static and type-level.
The discipline it invites ŌĆö model the validated value as a smart-constructed domain type so that passing the check is reflected in the type, not only in an event-log result ŌĆö is part of the FP enforcement layer, not something Dagster provides.

## 8. Sensors, schedules, and `AutomationCondition` are the effect pole ŌĆö outside BS├ĀlC

The exact API is `@sensor` (`decorators/sensor_decorator.py:39`, Python functions polled by the daemon emitting `RunRequest`s), `@schedule` (`decorators/schedule_decorator.py:42`, cron-triggered `RunRequest` emitters), and `AutomationCondition[T_EntityKey]` (`_core/definitions/declarative_automation/automation_condition.py:65`) attached per asset or check via `automation_condition=`.
`AutomationCondition` composes via `__and__` (`:240`), `__or__` (`:263`), `__invert__` (`:284`), and scoping operators `.since(...)` (`:291`) and `.allow(selection)` (boolean operators, e.g. `boolean_operators.py:101`); leaf factories include `missing()` (`:416`), `newly_updated()` (`:530`), `cron_tick_passed(...)`, `any_deps_match(...)`, `all_deps_match(...)`; prebuilt composites include `eager()` (`:783`) and `on_cron(...)` (`:805`).
Each condition exposes `children` (`:106`), `requires_cursor` (`:102`), and `operator_type` (`:120`), so a condition is a tree in a free algebra evaluated by `AutomationConditionEvaluator` against the asset graph, the event log, and a persisted cursor each daemon tick.

The categorical target is the impure driver ŌĆö the control/effect pole ŌĆö and it is deliberately *outside* the BS├ĀlC `Tasks` algebra.
Sensors are a process over a stream of world-states, modelable as a coalgebra `S ŌåÆ (Event, S)`: a stream transducer that observes external state and unfolds `RunRequest`s.
This is the seam where Dagster touches the effect pole; it is not part of the free term or its store interpreter, but the trigger that decides *when* the static asset graph is exercised.

`AutomationCondition` deserves a special note because it is *the single genuinely-algebraic surface in Dagster*.
Its `&`, `|`, `~` over leaf operands build syntactic `And`/`Or`/`Not` condition trees (`boolean_operators.py`), so the expression tree (`children`/`operator_type`) is literally a term in the *free* algebra over these operators ŌĆö and a free term algebra does not satisfy idempotence or absorption directly, since `A & A` is a distinct node from `A`.
The laws live at the semantic level: each condition's per-tick `evaluate` lands in the powerset boolean algebra of the candidate entity subset, where `&` computes intersection (`AndAutomationCondition.evaluate` via `compute_intersection`), `|` computes union (`compute_union`), and `~` computes the relative complement against the candidate subset (`compute_difference`).
That powerset algebra carries idempotence, absorption, and complement, so those laws hold after evaluation/normalization rather than on the unnormalized syntax tree ŌĆö which is exactly why normalization and simplification of automation conditions are algebraically meaningful operations.
But this is a boolean algebra *over entity state*, deciding which partitions of which entities to request ŌĆö it is not part of the `Tasks` algebra of entry 1, and treating it as such would conflate the effect pole with the build term.

This is *almost / outside-BS├ĀlC*: the boolean-algebra structure of `AutomationCondition` is real and unusually clean for Dagster, but sensors and schedules are honestly the other theory ŌĆö the impure scheduler-trigger bolted onto the side of the free-`Tasks` system.

## 9. Ops and graphs are morphisms in a free symmetric monoidal category

The exact API is `@op` (`_core/definitions/decorators/op_decorator.py:165`) producing an `OpDefinition` with positional `In`/`Out` ports, `@graph` (`graph_decorator.py:126`) producing a `GraphDefinition` by function-call composition (`composition.py`), and `GraphDefinition` itself as the composite node type.

The categorical target is a morphism in a free symmetric monoidal category, the string-diagram reading: ops are boxes, data dependencies are wires, sequential composition is morphism composition, and parallelism is the tensor product.
A `@graph` that wires ops by passing outputs as inputs is building a string diagram, and the op/graph layer is where Dagster is literally a free SMC ŌĆö the lowest, most syntactic layer of the model.

This is tight but legacy: the asset layer is the primary surface and lowers *to* the op/graph/job layer for execution, so the free-SMC structure is genuinely present but is the compilation target beneath assets rather than the layer a practitioner authors against directly.

## What 01 establishes and where the discipline lives

Entry by entry, this file establishes what maps to what: the asset graph is the free `Tasks` diagram (1), static-versus-dynamic is applicative-versus-monad (2), the IO manager is the store interpreter (3), partitions are an indexed family fibered over partition space (4), materialization plus `code_version` and `DataVersion` is the verifying-or-constructive trace with early cutoff (5), resources are a gestural reader monad (6), asset checks are a runtime-checked refinement (7), sensors and `AutomationCondition` are the effect-pole coalgebra and its boolean algebra (8), and ops and graphs are a free SMC (9).
The "almost" entries ŌĆö the IO-manager law, the resource discipline, the refinement-as-type discipline ŌĆö are exactly where the information is, because each names a coherence condition Dagster does not enforce.
This file says what-maps-to-what; *03-fp-discipline-and-enforcement.md* establishes the enforcement discipline and the fully worked lawful IO-manager example that makes the almosts lawful, and the Airflow-to-Flyte-v2-to-Dagster contrast on the BS├ĀlC constraint axis is the subject of *02-asset-vs-task-spectrum.md*.
