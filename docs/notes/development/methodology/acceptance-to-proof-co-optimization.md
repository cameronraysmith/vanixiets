This is a working note: a design thesis, authored 2026-07-02, guidance-only and conceptual rather than a roadmap, backlog, or tooling specification.
It synthesizes an architectural thesis the user has already accepted with a reconnaissance of the current apm skill corpus, scoped to the acceptance-to-proof spine of four skill packages: testing-and-quality (the acceptance pole), planning-and-development (the carrier), formal-specification-and-refinement (the proof pole), and preferences-functional-programming-theory.
Every claim about a present-day capability is held to the reconnaissance ground truth; every claim about the governing frame is presented as a synthesizing metaphor grounded in the in-repo refinement math, not as a running system.

## Two determinations of correct: validation and verification do not collapse

Correctness is not one property but two partially independent determinations, and the entire argument that follows depends on refusing to let them collapse into a single static source of truth.
Verification asks whether we built the thing right: whether the artifacts are internally consistent with the specification they descend from.
Validation asks whether we built the right thing: whether the specification is pointed at the region of the domain the stakeholders actually care about.
Wimsatt's robustness analysis names the reason to keep these apart, since a result trusted because several partially independent means of determination converge on it is more robust than one certified by a single channel, and treating verification and validation as one channel throws away exactly that independence.
The distinction is old and load-bearing in this stack: preferences-validation-assurance carries it as Boehm's V&V split and sharpens the verification side with Mayo's severity criterion, that a passing test is evidence only to the degree it would have failed under a plausibly incorrect implementation.
preferences-compositional-continuous-verification supplies the machine-checkable side of the split as an operating-envelope-plus-regulator discipline whose composed checks either close or expose a gap, and it is careful to distinguish its own compositional verification from the narrower runtime Continuous Validation that preferences-production-readiness owns.
preferences-scientific-inquiry-methodology grounds the whole loop in the Peircean triadic cycle of abduction, deduction, and induction, which is itself a refusal to collapse conjecture, consequence, and test into one act.
The acceptance pole encodes the same refusal operationally in Gate 1 of atdd-outer-loop, whose witnessing spectrum orders certificates by rigor precisely so that a low-rigor witness is never silently mistaken for a high-rigor one.
The thesis of this note is that co-optimizing acceptance and proof does not mean fusing these two determinations; it means running them as coupled but distinct loops and being honest at every step about which one a given artifact can and cannot certify.

## One domain model, two projections

Upstream of the four-package spine sits a single domain and event model, and the spine consumes it rather than owning it.
That model is the shared root cited by every corner of the spine and produced by the collaborative and event-modeling practices — preferences-collaborative-modeling, preferences-event-modeling, and preferences-domain-modeling — which this note treats strictly as an upstream boundary and does not re-derive.
The claim is that the same domain model admits two projections that look different but describe one thing.
One projection is the acceptance surface: the Gherkin scenarios of bdd-gherkin-formulation and the WHEN/THEN scenarios of the carrier's OpenSpec delta specs, each fixing an observable outcome in the language of a stakeholder.
The other projection is the verified surface: the algebraic data types of preferences-algebraic-data-types, the Wlaschin `Input -> Result<Output, Error>` workflow signatures of preferences-railway-oriented-programming, the property tests, and the Lean theorems.
Because both projections descend from one model they are functorial in the loose sense that structure in the domain maps coherently into each, and a seam where they touch is already visible in the skill corpus.
That seam is the Wlaschin signature: fixing `Input -> Result<Output, Error>` for a workflow simultaneously frames an acceptance scenario, since the scenario's observable outcome is exactly an inhabitant of the `Result` type, a success carrying an `Output` or a failure carrying a domain `Error`.
The one place this touching is concrete today is the observable-outcome discipline of bdd-gherkin-formulation, which insists a scenario's Then assert against an independent literal oracle that is itself an algebraic data value, `Ok([Deposited(50)])` or `Error(Overdraft())`, never re-deriving the expected value through the production path and never piercing private state behind the protocol.
That literal oracle is a hand-written ADT value, so the acceptance projection and the type projection genuinely meet there, in the shape of the asserted value, even though nothing yet mechanically checks that the two projections came from the same source.

## The quantifier-strength ladder: one rung per requirement

The stack renders one ladder four separate times under four different names, and this note fixes a single canonical name for it: the quantifier-strength ladder.
Its rungs are distinguished by the strength of the quantifier a certificate discharges.
The bottom rung is the example, an existential: there exists at least one input for which the observed outcome is the expected one, which is what a Gherkin scenario or a table-driven example test asserts.
The middle rung is the property test, a bounded universal: for all inputs within a generator's reach the property holds, which is what preferences-algebraic-laws asserts when it runs a law as an executable specification.
The top rung is the proved theorem, an unbounded universal: for all inputs whatsoever the proposition holds, which is what a Lean proof discharges.
preferences-functional-programming-theory instantiates this exact concrete-to-property-to-theorem progression end to end yet never names it as a ladder; atdd-outer-loop renders it as Gate 1's witnessing spectrum, where "a Lean proof witnesses more than a Hypothesis property, which witnesses more than a Gherkin scenario"; refinement-driven-development renders it as a graded check ladder over verification strength; preferences-validation-assurance renders it as the confidence-promotion chain from undemonstrated through validated to regression-protected.
The governing rule is one rung per requirement, chosen by risk and cost, not climb-everything-to-the-top.
A legible low-rigor certificate complements a high-rigor one and is often the right and only economical choice; the scenario that pins the right observable behavior is not made worthless by the absence of a theorem above it, and most requirements will and should terminate at the example or property rung.
Gate 1 of atdd-outer-loop is the router that performs the per-requirement selection, dispatching a behavioral proposition to BDD, a universal or algebraic law to a property test, a proof obligation to the Lean plus Charon and Aeneas round trip, a static-exhaustiveness claim to the type checker or lake build, and a symbolic edge case to CrossHair.
Naming the four renderings as one ladder is a synthesizing act; the renderings remain separately owned and, today, separately encoded.

## Co-optimization as a closure operator C = R after L

Model the co-optimization of acceptance and proof as maintaining a closure operator, C equal to R after L, sitting on a Galois connection between two lattices.
The behavioral-spec lattice B collects the OpenSpec delta-spec Requirements, each a MUST or SHALL statement carrying WHEN/THEN scenarios, together with the ATDD `.feature` scenarios, ordered by entailment and refinement.
The verified-artifact lattice A collects the algebraic data types, the Wlaschin `Result<Output, Error>` signatures, the property tests, the Lean theorems, and the passing checks, ordered by rigor and coverage.
The lowering map L from B to A takes a behavioral spec and produces artifacts: it fixes the `Input -> Result<Output, Error>` signature, an act that frames the scenario and authors the type in one motion, makes illegal states unrepresentable, and then chooses a rung per requirement by risk and cost, an invariant becoming a property test and a law or proof obligation becoming a Lean theorem.
So L is precisely Gate 1 routing composed with the type authoring of preferences-functional-programming-theory.
The reconstruction map R from A to B recovers the behavioral claims the artifacts actually witness or accept, and it has two named in-repo instances at different maturities: the reconstruction leg of the Nucleus round trip, realized today only as nix checks measuring structural drift rather than behavioral acceptance, and the lift leg of refinement-driven-development, specified via Charon and Aeneas carrying a Rust implementation back to its Lean specification but currently deferred.
Then C equal to R after L is the round trip on the behavioral-spec side, and a fixed point of C is a state where the spec you reconstruct from the artifacts equals the spec you set out to build: behavioral spec and types-and-proofs agree with zero drift.
This is not a parallel invention but the same object the proof pole already specifies, since Nucleus's nucleus O equal to R after L has fixed points that are its sheaves and refinement-driven-development's round trip Phi equal to alpha after gamma has the goal Phi approximately equal to the identity on Spec; the reframe lifts that existing closure math up to govern the entire acceptance-to-proof spine.
preferences-compositional-continuous-verification's operational closure operator, the composed nix flake check over the repository graph against pinned inputs, is then the computable shadow of C: it decides deterministically whether the state sits at, or within a declared tolerance of, a fixed point, even though it is an operational idempotent evaluation rather than the order-theoretic operator whose fixed points are closed sets.
Two honesty flags belong on this section.
The order-theoretic fixed point is the one genuinely net-new conceptual bridge here, and it is already latent in the RDD and Nucleus formulations rather than conjured from nothing; the formal construction of the two lattices and the proof that L and R form a Galois connection are the *target* of this framing, not something derived in this note.
And a fixed point of C certifies verification only: it says the artifacts agree with the spec they descend from, and it structurally cannot certify validation, because C only ever compares B against the A derived from it and never asks whether B was aimed at the right region of the domain, which is the separate determination Wimsatt keeps independent and which only the human loop can supply.

## Regime 1: initiation, greenfield bootstrap to the first fixed point

In the initiation regime no fixed point exists yet, and the task is to bootstrap one outside-in.
Work begins from the upstream domain and event model, the single model owned outside the spine whose two projections are the WHEN/THEN and Gherkin scenarios on one side and the ADTs and proofs on the other.
The outer loop derives acceptance scenarios first, which is a validation-shaped act, since choosing which observable outcomes to fix is choosing what "the right thing" means before any lowering has occurred.
Only then does L run, fixing the Wlaschin type signatures and the first tests, and the ATDD outer loop of atdd-outer-loop iterates its phases — discover, formulate, audit for spec leakage, gate on RED, run the inner test-driven-development cycle, gate on GREEN, refine, and verify — until a RED-to-GREEN transition produces the first artifacts whose reconstruction R returns the scenarios that were specified.
That agreement is the first fixed point.
The carrier realizes this as the OpenSpec change lifecycle driven through the planning-and-development skills: openspec-new-change opens the change, openspec-ff-change fast-forwards the eight superpowers-bridge artifacts, openspec-apply-change drives the apply-phase test-driven RED-GREEN work, and openspec-verify-change checks the result.
Validation enters this regime at two guarded points before any L is permitted to run: the brainstorming skill's hard gate, which forces an explicit exploration of intent and design before creative work begins, and the OpenSpec stop-and-wait discipline, at which a human confirms that the shape being committed to is the right thing.
Placing validation ahead of lowering is what keeps the first fixed point from being a well-built answer to the wrong question.

## Regime 2: iteration, re-closing a perturbed fixed point under a regulator

In the iteration regime a fixed point already exists and something perturbs it: a new requirement, a refactor, a dependency bump, or a discovered severity gap.
Any such perturbation is exactly the "regressed" demotion of preferences-validation-assurance's confidence-promotion chain, a previously validated or regression-protected artifact knocked back down the chain.
The loop re-closes by re-running L on the delta and re-running R through the checks and the verify step until C returns identity again.
Where re-closing all the way to a proof-strength fixed point is infeasible — and by Rice's theorem together with Ashby's law of requisite variety perfect coverage is structurally impossible, not merely expensive — the discipline is not to demand a proof but to install a regulator so that the gap between intended and exercised behavior stays visible, bounded, and decreasing.
This is the operating-envelope-plus-regulator construction of preferences-compositional-continuous-verification, in which property tests and acceptance scenarios serve as the regulator's sensors, and under it C degrades gracefully from an exact fixed point at the proof rung to a regulated approximate fixed point at the property or scenario rung within a declared envelope.
The two notions of regulator in this stack are one notion under the Conant-Ashby good-regulator theorem: preferences-compositional-continuous-verification's verification regulator, which holds a verification envelope, and preferences-adaptive-planning's control regulator, its model-predictive receding-horizon replanner and its Viable System Model System 3-star validation-gate audit, are both good regulators of the system they govern precisely because every good regulator of a system is a model of that system, which gives the iteration regime a single coherent regulator concept spanning verification and control.
preferences-adaptive-planning also supplies the scheduler: its model-predictive receding-horizon replanning schedules re-closure work, replanning when accumulated surprise crosses a threshold at a DAG convergence point, so that the loop maximizes the rate of validated increments rather than chasing closure everywhere at once.
Validation re-enters at the human-in-the-loop checkpoint, the In Review board state, where the carrier writes verify.md, runs the roborev codex advisory gate, and runs the documenter, mirroring the transition to Linear; the human at In Review asks whether the change is still the right thing, the one question C cannot answer, and termination is a bounded-retries re-queue, failing back to In Progress for roughly three rounds before escalating.

## The carrier today: how the spine actually runs

Described concretely and in the present tense, the carrier is the only thing binding the two poles, and it binds them operationally rather than conceptually.
Acceptance criteria enter as OpenSpec delta-spec Requirements whose text must contain SHALL or MUST, each carrying WHEN/THEN scenarios with no explicit GIVEN field, the given state left implicit, and each scenario labeled a potential test case.
The superpowers-bridge schema sequences eight artifacts, brainstorm, proposal, design, specs, tasks, plan, verify, and retrospective, and the agentic-planning-development-workflow router walks a change across a Linear-canonical board through an AFK, HIL, or Manual execution-mode fork.
The apply phase invokes subagent-driven-development, which enforces the test-driven-development RED-GREEN-REFACTOR cycle and then requesting-code-review; openspec-verify-change follows, checking completeness, correctness, and coherence and writing verify.md as pass, warn, or fail; the roborev codex advisory gate and the documenter run next; and archive then syncs the delta specs into openspec/specs/, which is the single source of truth for accepted behavior.
Linear, driven through linear-cli rather than the disabled MCP, is the human-visible projection, mirrored on four file-anchored lifecycle transitions by openspec-linear-sync.
Every advance into and through In Review requires human adjudication, and the codex advisory is itself nondeterministic, so the running system already treats the validation determination as a distinct human channel.
The precise state of the binding is worth stating plainly: acceptance is linked to the types projection weakly and concretely, through the ADT-literal observable-outcome oracles and Gate 1's static-exhaustiveness route into the type checker, and it is linked to the proofs projection only by a named-but-unworked routing edge in Gate 1 plus a dangling verified-Lean-oracle promise, so in the running system acceptance and proof meet in the carrier's apply-phase TDD and verify steps and essentially nowhere else.
beads is dormant here: it remains woven through the Manual-mode ledger and the router initialization but is deprecated, and the live human-in-the-loop path is openspec to superpowers-bridge to Linear.

## The proof pole: reality and its ceiling

The proof pole is architecturally specified and locally demonstrated, and it is important not to read the closure-operator thesis as a claim that proof-strength fixed points are routinely reachable.
What is real is threefold.
Lean 4 is the structural source of truth, and its executable-decidable fragment genuinely lets example witnesses via `#eval` and `#guard` co-reside with for-all decidable theorems in one source, demonstrated at small scale in preferences-theoretical-foundations, where Limit.lean proves `project_hom` as a monoid-homomorphism theorem, a corollary of `fmap_hom`, with no sorry and no added axiom, while limit.py at lines 217 to 219 asserts the very same law as a runtime property.
That Limit.lean and limit.py pairing is the one honest end-to-end three-rung artifact in the stack, example beside property beside theorem for a single law.
The nucleus itself is fully specified as intended mechanism: refinement-driven-development's adjunction of alpha lifting against gamma lowering with its round trip Phi approximately equal to the identity on Spec and dual closure gamma after alpha contained in the identity on Impl, and nucleus-platform's O equal to R after L as a Lawvere-Tierney nucleus that is idempotent, inflationary, and meet-preserving with fixed points that are sheaves and residual that is holonomy.
What is not real yet bounds the thesis.
Within the Nucleus instance the theorem side is largely deferred, with Pipeline morphism bodies left as sorry and the Spec and Refinement proofs and the strict Charon-and-Aeneas reconstruction deferred; the realized reconstruction gates are nix checks measuring structural holonomy only, explicitly excluding the LinkML semantic axis, so their "correctness" is structural drift and not behavioral acceptance; the Lean-side spec-versus-lifted-model differential testing is described as the prescribed design for the loop, not something already wired; reference-corpus theorems often ship as axiom declarations; no Nucleus instance has been witnessed end to end; the refinement order is imported framing with no in-repo operator; and mechanical proof is Rust-only through Charon and Aeneas, so Python and TypeScript-hosted requirements top out at the differential, property, and human tiers.
This pole holds zero references to the BDD and ATDD family, grep-confirmed, so the acceptance-scenario-to-theorem join is unbuilt from the proof side.
The examples-beside-theorems capability is therefore real as a Lean feature and as documented methodology but aspirational as an integrated acceptance-to-proof pipeline, and the full acceptance-scenario-to-Lean-theorem route and the Nucleus round trip closing to a proof-strength fixed point are the asymptotic target the closure operator points at rather than a current capability.
Consistent with that, preferences-functional-programming-theory disclaims its own QMADTT synthesis as conjectural, stating it "does not yet exist as one built or proven calculus; it is a conjectural synthesis," and that self-disclaimer is the honest ceiling for the whole frame.

## The boundary of the closure today

What follows is descriptive, a faithful account of where C reaches today and where it does not, and it deliberately prescribes no tooling to extend that reach.
The two poles never reference each other in either direction, so there is no machine-checked link between an acceptance scenario and a type or a theorem; the carrier is the only binder and it binds operationally through apply-phase TDD and verify, never conceptually.
The quantifier-strength ladder exists four times over — the concrete-property-theorem progression of preferences-functional-programming-theory, the witnessing spectrum of atdd-outer-loop, the graded check ladder of refinement-driven-development, and the confidence-promotion chain of preferences-validation-assurance — with no shared name in the running corpus and no machine-checked linkage across the four, so binding one requirement simultaneously to a scenario witness, a property witness, and a proof witness with a promotion path between them is a composition that does not exist; the @req-<slug> traceability tags map requirement to scenario only.
The carrier's OpenSpec WHEN/THEN spec.md and the ATDD `.feature` scenarios are two encodings of one acceptance layer living in different homes and confusingly both spoken of as "spec.md," with no bridge between them; this note treats them as one layer conceptually and plants no pointer toward any generator or witness-composition mechanism that would join them.
The verified-Lean-oracle route for a Gherkin Then is named exactly once, at bdd-gherkin-formulation SKILL line 148, and no reference shows how it works, so the observable-outcome corpus remains hand-written Python literals and the proof link stays a dangling promise; make-illegal-states-unrepresentable is not linked into the acceptance surface at all.
The shared upstream root, the domain and event model that every corner cites, is owned outside the four-package spine, so even the common origin of the two projections is not something the spine itself holds.
These joints are the current boundary of the closure: real as operational or conceptual relationships, absent as machine-checked links, and named here only to mark honestly how far C reaches, not to specify how to push it further.
